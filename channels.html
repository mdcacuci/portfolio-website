My Channels

BrainSTEMs

BrainSTEMs is my YouTube channel focused on teaching the basics and fundamentals of STEM as well as making fun and engaging videos for all ages. The goal of BrainSTEMs is to introduce kids and teens with a limited experience in STEM to the interesting and exciting world of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math.

Romance Op. 24 No. 9 by Jean Sibelius

Jean Sibelius was a Finnish late Romantic and Early-Modern composer. Sibelius's musical influences included Tchaikovsky, Busoni, Bruckner, Liszt, and particularly Wagner. Sibelius often deviated from sonata form and contrasting mutiple themes and instead focused on evolving fragments that eventually culminated in a grand statement.

The Mathematics of Music

When you think of math, what do you think of? Multiplication tables, formulas, shapes? Well, certainly not music! In this video I go over the interesting connection between math and music, and how the creativity and freedom of art communicates and interacts with the logicality and orderliness of math.

Toccata by Aram Khachaturian

Aram Khachaturian was an Armenian and Soviet composer and conductor. Khachaturian's works span a broad range of genres, from symphonies and concertos to ballets and film scores. Khachaturian's music is often characterized by an active rhythmic development, which drives the piece and creates a sharply identifiable style.

Vanished Days Op. 57 No. 1 by Edvard Grieg

Edvard Grieg was a Norwegian Romantic composer, known for helping to develop a Norwegian national identity through his use of Norweigian folk music in his compositions. Grieg's works are noted for their refined and novel lyrical sense and spirited rhythms that show his characteristic sense of rhythm and harmony.
